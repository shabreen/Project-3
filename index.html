Tweet Sentiment Analysis

The above graph shows the volume and sentiment of tweets mentioning each candidate on May 2nd. Tweets are grouped by sentiment - positive, neutral, or negative - and by candidate. Each group is then plotted with a bubble whose size is proportional to the number of tweets. The vertical position of each bubble indicates the average sentient score of the underlying tweets, and a horizontal dotted line is plotted at the average score of all tweets mentioning a candidate.

Analysis:

Negative tweets about Kamala Harris have an usually low average score. Examining the tweets directly, we found that President Trump had tweeted critically of her recently, calling her "Nasty Kamala Harris" after she aggressively questioned Attorney General William Barr at a public Senate hearing. Many Trump supporters used the 'nasty' appellation in tweets about Harris on May 2nd, and these tweets scored particularly low in our model's scoring. A smaller number of Harris supporters used the same language while quoting Trump, resulting in negatively-scored tweets which were actually supportive of Harris. Removing these tweets or adjusting their score could have been explored with more time.
